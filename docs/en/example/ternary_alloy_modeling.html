Surface modeling of ternary alloy

Here, we will explain the method for extending to ternary alloys.

Alloys composed of three or more metals, including ternary alloys, are widely used as functional materials such as stainless steel and materials for hydrogen production.

Compared to pure metals and binary alloys, while it is possible to control the structure and properties of alloy surfaces more extensively, it is considered difficult to elucidate their origins.

This page describes the differences when modeling ternary alloys as an application example of SurfQit to multi-component alloys.

1. Set the Calculation Parameter via Config File

Basically, the target alloy system can be handled by specifying it during the initial config settings.

 1import allfunc
 2config = allfunc.Prepare_Config()
 3config.read()
 4
 5#Exection location
 6config.write("preparation","storage_path","./work/")
 7allfunc.storage_path_initialize(config)
 8
 9#Target alloy composition
10config.write("preparation","substitute_ratio",[{"Cu": [0.0, 1.0], "Co": [0.0, 1.0], "Pd": [0.0, 1.0]}])
11
12#Surface model size
13config.write("preparation","supercell_multiple",[3,3,3])
14
15#Path to the CIF file of the initial bulk structure
16config.write("preparation","host_path",'./bulk.cif')
17
18#For check
19config.read()

At this point, surface modeling of various multi-component alloys can be performed by changing the type of metals or the number of elements.

Before and after this section, you can proceed by executing in the same way as in Surface modeling of Cobalt-Copper alloy .
